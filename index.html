Reflection

Refelcting on the course and how I developed over the term

  • Beginnings of the course


          At the beginning of the course I thought my perspective on communication would stay the same, that being engineers don't communicate very  well . That being said, after participating in all the in class activities and doing the assignments, I can happily say that my opinion has changed. The content of the course is straightforward, but at the same time, hits to the  core of what makes a good engineer. How communication was defined and analyzed made the concepts of communicating easy to understand and easy to apply.

  • Using the tools

    taught in class


            The most striking instance of these conceps  were the axioms of communication. This set of tools alone made the course all the more engaging and worth it in a sense. I feel this way because these communication axioms were the building blocks that built up my current communication skills. The hardest roadblock to get over while learning to harness these skills is best described in axiom 6 . Being that engineering is a competitive field, engaging with others sometimes was tough as I didn't want to be compared to or judged. Using the class activities and axioms really helped not only myself but the people  around me. We all became better communicators together and helped each other when we needed it. This was probably the best outcome from this course that I could have possibly anticipated from this course. I became more comfortable while presenting material and talking within groups or to individuals became easier and clearer.

  • Cataloguing

    My Growth


            To catalogue my growth as a communicator, the best way would be to compare my presentations for the technical report and the back of the napkin lesson. These assignments both required similar but different approaches to get right. My back of the napkin was completed first so it encompases a less mature understanding of communication whereas the technical presentation was done later. The most growth I experienced was because of the fact that my presentation wasn't selected for the group as a whole to present(assignment 6). Growth came of this  because I needed to be engaged with the creator of the document in order to really understand what is going on. In contrast to the back of the napkin assignment, I competed that myself and thus used my own ideas and way of thinking to communicate my message. The challenge in this case was to use my communication skills well enough to the point where I ask the right questions to my group mates and keep the integrity of the project intact. By the time it came to record, I notice now that I better communicate my ideas in assignment 6 where as in assignment 5 I could have done better.

  • Continuous 

    Improvement


     Also another area that improved was my ability to tailor my communication to the desired audience. From assignment 5 to assignment 6 I noticed that I used more clear and concise language to better communicate my point. Since we are talking to engineers, I was able to better transform my style to one that is both underlyingly technical but relatable at the same time. This will probably become an invaluable skill that makes me an even more effective communicator to any audience I come across.
